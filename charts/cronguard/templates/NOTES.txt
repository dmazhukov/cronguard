  kubectl -n {{ .Release.Namespace }} get deploy {{ $fullname }}
  kubectl -n {{ .Release.Namespace }} logs deploy/{{ $fullname }} -c manager

Apply a sample CronJobMonitor (after applying or having a CronJob):

  apiVersion: monitoring.cronguard.io/v1alpha1
  kind: CronJobMonitor
  metadata:
    name: my-job
  spec:
    cronJobRef:
      name: my-cronjob
    schedule: "0 2 * * *"
    maxConsecutiveFailures: 2
    alertAfterMissedRuns: 2

Then watch its status:

  kubectl get cronjobmonitor
  kubectl describe cronjobmonitor my-job
